Algorithms

Algorithms are already all around us. Algorithms are what drives Google’s search engine. Algorithms are what power Netflix to figure out what we want to watch next, Amazon Alexa’s voice assistants, Tinder’s match-making, driverless cars, high-speed trading and an ever-growing number of services and technologies.

After all, what makes Google or Facebook, one of the most valuable brands in the world isn’t the massive data. it’s the company’s most closely guarded secret, its algorithms.

While algorithms have been essential in helping improve our lives, boost world’s economy and rapid advancements in science and technology, they also have inadvertently increased huge inequality in society. Biased algorithms are growing every day and neither the companies developing them nor the government is interested in addressing the problem.

Virginia Eubanks, a political scientist and author of the book ‘Automating Inequality’ writes in her book, how using computers to decide who gets social services is hurting the poor. How some of these high-tech tools are making inequality worse with automated systems that control which neighborhoods get policed, which families attain needed resources, and who is investigated for fraud.
